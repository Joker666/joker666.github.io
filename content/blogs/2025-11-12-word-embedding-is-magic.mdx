When I first learned about word embedding models, something fell off. How can a computer understand language? It turns out, it can't. But it can approximate it. We train a model to predict nearby words. Given "credit" the model tries to predict "card". But here's the thing: nobody actually cares about this prediction task. We want a model to understand the relationship between these two words. I've used word embedding models without fully understanding how it works. And to scratch this itch, I found one of the most profound inventions, at least to my eyes. It is like magic.

> We train the model on a task we intend to fail at (or at least, a task we don't plan to use)
